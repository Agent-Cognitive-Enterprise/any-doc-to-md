## Supplies

- • Confirm patient identity against the wristband and appointment record.
. Prepare the sterile tray before the clinician enters the room.
.. Label the specimen bag with patient initials, visit date, and tray number.

The intake lead verifies that the room is stocked before the first appointment.
If a supply is unavailable, the lead documents the shortage in the room log and
uses the approved substitute list rather than improvising.

## Patient Verification

The coordinator asks the patient to state their full name and date of birth.
Insurance, consent, allergy, and translator needs are checked before the
clinical workflow begins. These checks are short, but they are important because
the rest of the packet assumes that the identity and consent steps are complete.

The source packet also includes a short caution about conflicting demographic
records. That caution is not reproduced here, which should be treated as a
coverage problem by an auditor.

## Specimen Handling

Specimen bags are staged on the side table until the clinician confirms that the
sample has been collected. The assistant seals the bag, reads the label back to
the clinician, and records the handoff time in the intake worksheet.

When the patient requires a translator, the translator name and session number
are recorded next to the consent status. Missing translator evidence should be
flagged during review because it can invalidate a downstream audit trail.

## Safety Notes

The room should be reset after every encounter. Gloves and wipes are discarded
immediately, the work surface is cleaned from the far edge toward the door, and
the next tray is opened only after the surface is dry.

The source document explains that wet surfaces can contaminate adhesive labels.
That detail matters because labels applied to damp bags may detach during
transport and break chain-of-custody evidence.

## Shift Handoff

At the end of the shift, the lead compares the intake worksheet against the
sample transport manifest. Exceptions are summarized in the handoff note and
sent to the next shift lead before the room is closed.

A large source-only paragraph about escalation timing, wet-label replacement,
and chain-of-custody correction is missing from this candidate.
