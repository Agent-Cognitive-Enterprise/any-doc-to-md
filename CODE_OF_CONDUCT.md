# ADTM Code Of Conduct

## Our Standard

ADTM is an engineering project. Productive disagreement is welcome; contempt is
not.

Participants are expected to:

- critique ideas, code, benchmarks, and assumptions without attacking people
- be precise about evidence, reproduction steps, and tradeoffs
- respect different levels of experience without lowering the technical bar
- keep discussions focused on improving the project and helping others learn
- avoid harassment, discrimination, intimidation, and personal abuse

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- personal attacks, insults, or sustained hostile behavior
- sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- harassment based on identity, background, disability, religion, nationality,
  or similar protected characteristics
- doxxing, threats, stalking, or publishing private information without consent
- deliberate disruption of discussions, review, or community processes
- knowingly posting malware, credential material, or private documents

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- issue trackers
- pull requests and code review
- discussions and maintainer decisions
- release, benchmark, and support conversations in project-controlled spaces

## Reporting

If you experience or witness a serious conduct issue, contact the maintainers
through the private security/contact path described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

When reporting, include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots if available
- whether there is an immediate safety or privacy concern

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove content, moderate discussions, reject contributions, or
restrict participation when behavior violates this policy.

The goal is not ritual process. The goal is a technically rigorous project that
people can safely contribute to.
